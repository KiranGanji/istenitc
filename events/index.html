About Us

The Indian Society for Technical Education ISTE was founded in the year 1967 with a mission to advance for the cause of education in general and all its function which pertain to engineering and allied branches of engineering and technology, architecture and town planning, pharmacy, management, process of teaching and learning, research, extension services and public relations in particular. The main goal is to serve as a common agency for stimulating and guiding its members to formulate the general goals and responsibilities of technical education for the service of mankind and the advancement of general welfare. Having started its chapter in NITC in 2008 it has evolved and grown through leaps and bounds to become one of the most sought after and looked upon groups of the campus. The chapter organizes quite many events many of them exclusively for the first years. This includes AKS-Abdul Kalam Scholarship test which had always had a huge turnout. We have spectrum a technical fest for them. In addition to which we host inter college fests such as i-CON. We also organize industrial visits and career workshops for the students of our college. Besides these we also involve earnestly in the works of tathva the technical fest of our college. Every year we elect our new chairman, vice-chairman, treasurer by taking into account the interests and opinion of everyone who is a part of the chapter. We work as a team and believe in betterment through teamwork

AKS

Abdul Kalam Scholarship(AKS) The Abdul Kalam Scholarship(AKS) is the premier scholarship examination conducted by ISTE exclusively for the first years. Format: The AKS is divided into three stages based on which the judgement is made- Written test, Group Discussion (GD) and a Personal Interview. SAT & SET: The first written stage is the screening stage where all the candidates have to write two examinations, the SAT (Scholastic Aptitude Test) and the SET (Special Entrance Test),both of which every candidate must attempt in one sitting. The SAT is designed to test the Student's Aptitude through a set of brain-racking questions. The SET basically consists of questions from the four basic fields of science: Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry and Computer Science. It is a completely objective, multiple-choice question paper which is designed to check upon the candidate's flair in the four branches of science. The papers will be evaluated and the top 10% of students (with weightage being given to both the papers)will be selected. GD: The Qualifying Scholars from the Written test will be asked to discuss on topics given by the panel of judges.The student's knowledge of concepts and his/her communication skills is evaluated in this round. Further shortlisting ensues and successful candidates move on to the next round. PI: Now, these AK Scholars will have to undergo the PI (Personal Interview) round. In this round, the Scholars are suppose to face grilling by our interview panel which is further sub-divided into two parts, one comprising of 2nd and 3rd years and the other comprising of 4th years. The questions shot at the Scholars will be general questions based upon the CVs and also there will be questions from which we will judge the candidate's perspective of looking at certain situations. Eventually, after all these rounds, we will select candidates for the top three positions and those scholars holding the first, second and third positions will be awarded with cash prize of Rs. 3500, Rs. 2500 and Rs. 1500 respectively. "Participating in AKS was the single most important step I took in my first year. And it turned out to be very useful." -NEERAJ P J, Winner , AKS'10s

Spectrum

Theory is when you know something and it doesn't work, practice is when something works and you don't know why... Engineers combine theory and practise Nothing works and they don't know why!!!

Spectrum is an inter branch event conducted exclusively for the first year students of NITC. ISTE NITC Student Chapter through the past series of this event successfully inculcated a sense of camaraderie, team spirit, leadership and branch spirit among the first years. It brought to the fore the technical acumen of the students through a series of events and tests to test their technical skills and capabilities. It is the institute’s second biggest technical event only next to TATHVA (the institute’s techno-management fest) and is backed by potential sponsors such as PIZZA CORNER and CADD centre. On the basis of the performance of the first years in the events in spectrum, apprentices are selected for ISTE after a personal interview. Held in Jan-Feb every year, this event witnesses over 500 first year students battling it out to prove themselves as the best in the year.

Industrial Visit
